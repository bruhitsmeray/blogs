A tiny intro

Hi, today I want to take a look at my journey of becoming a programmer so far, starting somewhere in 2019 and finishing with what to expect in the future.

2020

For the people that didn't follow me back in 2020 (which is most of you), this is kind of a recreation of an older blog post of mine. A post so old that the design of the blog back then resembled more of a Twitter post than it did a blog post.


In that respective post, I was talking about procrastination and my love for Cyberpunk 2077, a game that still stuck around with me until this day, despite public reception at the time. I also talked about the struggles of streaming on Twitch.


I ended the blog on quite a negative note if you ask me, 2020 was a hard year for everyone and I didn't know what 2021 had in store for me.

2021

On 21st of November, 2021, I've posted a short blog named "One year later" in which I've talked about how 2021 is nearly over, the blog post redesign and some of the newly started projects.


A day later I've postead a new blog named "Let's catch up again". I do remember not having inspiration for a new name for the blog so I just used the same name as in 2020 but slightly changed. In this blog post I've talked about some updates made to my then in-development Discord bot named "Eutera" and about Decay and some other projects.

2022

On June 14th, 2022, I've posted a blog named "A new beginning" in which I've mostly just wanted to say "Hi" and talked a bit about some Unreal Engine plugins that I was working on at the time.

2023

Programming is an amazing and creative journey, I don't regret taking this step and I can't wait to create lots of cool stuff.Since last time I did a "Catch up" blog I've started telling y'all about my projects. I'll do this again now, for old time's sake.

Eutera

"Eutera" is kinda broken in 2023, and I can't be bothered to fix it because I'm working on a big upgrade. I promise I'll let y'all know when that is ready, but for now I have some more important projects to deal with, so "Eutera" doesn't have a date when it comes to the next release (for now).

Aleph

"Aleph" went through a strange evolution over the years, from a base of future projects and games, to a standalone game. In 2023, "Aleph" is a co-op game, in early development, and yes, more early than "Decay".


I don't really have anything to say more than that. It still exists. That's it for now, next.

HyperionCore & HyperionUI

Well these are new. HyperionCore & HyperionUI are two Unreal Engines plugins that I've started developing in Late 2022 and are what "Aleph" was meant to be in the beginning.


HyperionCore handles the logic of a game while HyperionUI handles the UI. I've made these plugins to help beginner game devs prototype their ideas faster. I'll make a video showcase after I spend some more time on developing them.


I promise it will be worth it, I'm really excited about these two.

Decay

Last but not least, "Decay", I can't wait to get back to work on this one. It slowly grows into a beauty, I'm still prototyping stuff, but everytime I get to work on it, it gets better and better. Hopefully by Christmas I'll have a demo ready for y'all to try.

Goodbye.. for now

Wow.. this blog was a long one but I really wanted to take a look back on my journey so far and show you what there is to look forward to.


For now, I wish y'all a great day and thanks for reading this! I'll see you later, goodbye!
